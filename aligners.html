Possible treatment needed

IPR

Stands for Interproximal Reduction also known as "enamel reduction". IPR is commonly used to repair crowded teeth for braces or aligners treatment. It's required to remove little portions of the outermost enamel from multiple teeth in spots that were crowded or surround crowded teeth.

Attachments

Tiny tooth-colored “bumps” that are bonded to the teeth using dental composites. They will be placed at precise locations to allow the braces to grip the teeth, providing additional force to guide the teeth to their new locations

Elastic/Rubber bands

A small band that attaches to the braces to assist the patient's teeth movement in specific directions to correct bite conditions like overbite & underbite.

Aligners Do's and Don'ts

Unlike braces, aligners are removable, so there are some precautions that we need to take when it comes to eating habits and maintenance.

  • Do wear your aligners 20-22 hours/day
  • Do keep track of the schedule of your aligners (change to a new set every 10 days)
  • Do rinse your mouth after every meal
  • Do store your aligners in your aligners case
  • Do clean your aligners with cleaning tablet every day
  • Do not drink hot water with aligners on
  • Do not eat without removing your aligners
  • Do not use hot water or mouthwash to clean your aligners
  • Do not brush your aligners with toothpaste
  • Do not skip any single day of wearing aligners
